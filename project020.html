SPECULATIVE ARCHITECTURE

2046: How did Grinch Brought Christmas back?



"I am interested in how the world will end."

Or if one prefers more optimistically– how would we possibly steer the world’s trajectory?

This collection of materials discusses the intangible topic of thinking: focusing particularly on perspectival shifts in perceiving and processing information. The exploration stems from a strong belief (or assumption) that ideology has determinant impacts on the future of the Earth. Beginning with decisions and actions by individual beings, which then interrelate with different entities: human, nonhuman, living, non-living — ideology sets off a series of interconnected events that ultimately shape a different story ending for the entire system, slowly yet drastically. This index hopes to stimulate insight, or rather an altered speculation of being in the future world as a part of the human collective.

We are in an era where originality is scarce, creativity and ideas are saturated and to some extent in stalemate. Kolatan’s chunk models suggest that intriguing chemistry happens when odd combinations of the unfamiliar are put together. Ursula’s carrier bag theory proposes that we examine every character and stories happening in the world instead of isolating the most prominent ones to tell. Feminist authors write about the fault in immediacy and linearity when it comes to how we look at things. They all point to ‘a new way of thinking’ which I believe is of utmost importance in this era.
