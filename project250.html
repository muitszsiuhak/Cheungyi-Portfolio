For the First Time:

Encountering the Enchanting Nature

Instructor: David Ruy | Assistant Teacher: Esin Karaosman



" We address each other with names, but our treatment of other beings is strikingly different. "

This collection, spanning photographs, taxidermies, narratives, and graphs,
unsettles our distinctions between the familiar and the other, weaving existing realities through a speculative fiction of hybrid creatures.


The Collection

This exhibition showcases eight fictional hybrid creatures, each unsettlingly familiar – imbuing a sense of eerie. These creations are not entirely fantastical but emerge from what is 'already here'. They are existing images of our everyday encounterment that are taxidermied into something that resembles animals. The collection proposes that oddities might have always been lying dormant within the ordinary – here, unraveled through artificial intelligence.


'Nectomoebus': the Parrot-Lobster-Crab

This is a physical taxidermy of one out of the eight critters, encased in a Victorian-style frame. It has a parrot's torso, embedded in a fleshy, almost slimy body. Its pair of rough scaly claws gently lay on top of the wool-like fluff that is competing to fill the body,and spiky bright-red crab legs extend outwards. Its realism, almost liveliness, captivates audiences to befriend with itself. Yet its clinical look calls for careful observations, dreading viewers to reframe the familiar and uncover the strangeness hidden within.


The Bestiary

Each animal is accompanied by a narrative that builds on its discovery, origin, and behavior. This one for example, is the Clangorvis. It looks like a fiery kangaroo that has an apple-stalk-like abdomen. This bony abdomen apparatus acts as a resonant chamber and disrupts other aerial animals' communication. Another creature, Abyssophryx, uses the scavenged head of marine corpses as camouflage, anchoring its true body beneath riverbeds seeking protection for its vulnerable torso. These imaginative backstories capture the enchantment of encountering nature for the first time in an alternative perspective, reminding us of its endless capacity to surprise.


The Strata

Like drawers at the backhouses of a natural history museum, science knowledge is traditionally constructed through hierarchical taxonomic tree diagrams that segregation rather than interconnectivity. This behavioral map here, instead displays a quality of engagement and entanglement amongst the discovered animals, akin chutes and ladders. It does not seek to localize creatures at a certain accurate point, or come up with a unified theory to explain their existences, instead to showcase the undefinable complexity of nature. A sense of infinite expansion is suggested as well by the list.




A NEW AESTHETIC OF NATURE

This imaginative yet real fiction does not propose a new science but instead offers a speculative lens to the existing worlds that we inhabit — as if the antennae that peeks out of the ground, for us to view nature in another perspective - one that celebrates the hidden, the odd, and the endlessly fascinating.
