WRITING / PARAFICTION

'Alice Through the Looking-Glass'



"When I look at you, I see a young little girl, living her life being told she's too weird." --D.R."


So, here's how I first made the acquaintance of Alice, when she recounted to me all her even-more-absurd encounters upon returning from the most perplexing realm of the Wonderland...


"Oh, but implausibility, my darling, is merely a symptom of insufficient curiosity! Let me see... it began upon a frozen river, a Frost Fair unlike any London has seen, with a great bamboo scaffold Christmas tree rising from the ice. Immense machines slid down rails—gigantic copper brewers puffing spiced mist, a grand dining hall encased within a ten-story Victorian table... gingerbread puppet house that whispered when wind passed through it."


“Let me get this straight, Alice. You mean to tell me you had seen singing plants and dancing bamboos, and gave a raddish a fresh haircut..." "...That having braved the preposterous absurdities of Wonderland— you wandered further still, into realms yet more implausible, now what a trout factory that makes motherboards?"


"Whispering gingerbread. Preposterous," I mocked. "I had barely grasped the wonder of it when I found myself at the foot of a crystal tower, its facets shifting, folding like paper origamis. A young loving couple emerged, wreathed in the glow of something unutterable, elevating from one reality into another as if it were no more than a doorway." "And yet you stand there, utterly unfractured by such disturbances in the laws of physics—"


"Physics is but a territorial rulebook, my dear, and the Wonderlands does not abide by its limitations! I wandered through a forest and intruded the haven of ancient Ammonites, lay curled up, glowing... and elf-like suspended trunks exhaled tiny creatures into the air. A wooden stool, fashioned like a scorpion, scuttled to my side and, finding me sufficiently agreeable, bore me away..." "So then to where on earth, did this whimsical steed deliver you?" I feigned a semblance of intrigue, inwardly remaining most unconvinced. "An island woven of synthetic blooms, their iridescence hair swayed with each breath of wind. But the beasts, oh, the beasts! A parrot where its crab legs squirmed, a deer with a body of fluff-filled chestnut, a frog draped in peacock plumage—each a wonder unto itself! And as I marveled, a great machine resembling a water treatment plant, all pipes and pulsing fluid, howled from stories above, leading me toward a conservatory where tunes chimed from potted plants wired with uncanny devices." "Okay now a botanical orchestra. Don't you try me, Alice—" "Yet you listen still! But I shall spare you the unraveling of an impossible haunting cathedral and the tentacled beast slumbered within it—for now. What I shall tell you is this: the final thing I saw before I was whisked back to this tiresome realm was a disjoint dragon, with metal bladed scales and bright red glowing eyes, mimicking my every move. I stepped, it stepped. I turned, it turned. I ran—" And though her heart raced, Alice did not flee. She had come this far. And Wonderland—if this world could still be called that—had only just begun to unfold......
