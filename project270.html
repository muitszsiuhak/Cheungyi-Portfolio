SCULPTURE

HybridVeg 1.0, 2.0



HybridVeg explores the manipulation of vegetables across their different stages of life and decay—rawness, ripeness, and preservation—while staging unlikely encounters between organic and synthetic materials. Through playful interventions with eggplant, cucumber, radish, and ginger, the project foregrounds kinships that emerge not only from their shared textures and colors but also from their potential to blur categories between animal, vegetable, and artificial matter.



Research Images: Japanese Leak


By binding, wrapping, and grafting vegetables with ropes, fabrics, and packaging plastics, the work situates them in a liminal state: neither purely food nor object, neither specimen nor artifact. These assemblages echo museological displays and scientific preservation, but with an irreverent twist that reveals their vulnerability, humor, and strangeness.

At its core, HybridVeg is a meditation on how material manipulation—cutting, wrapping, piercing, binding—can re-enchant the familiar. Vegetables, often dismissed as background materials for human sustenance, are re-presented as sculptural entities that evoke empathy, discomfort, and kinship.

What happens when the everyday objects of consumption are treated as living beings, or even as speculative hybrids—somewhere between animal and plant, natural and synthetic?
