HUMANITIES / SPECULATIVE FICTION / ARCHITECTURE

Taste of the Living

Graduate Thesis

Instructor: David Ruy | Assistant Teacher: Esin Karaosman



"When a poultry becomes a meal, it is of course dead."

Yet when the meal is still in the form of its raw material, the chicken meat for example, it somehow still feels alive. Where does this lingering sense of life come from? Perhaps it lies in the aesthetic qualities of rawness itself: in the primal and visceral materiality that resists full abstraction. This thesis is a curation of Grand Central Market into an theatrical experience that reminds forms of desire that touches the primal.


Grand Central Market: A History of 'Polite Pretentiousness'

In the heart of Los Angeles, this place once embodied a similar liveliness. As a marketplace, it thrived on sensory immediacy encompassing fresh groceries. Animated negotiations and selection around them created a communal aliveness regarding survival. Today, the “market” remains but operates as a lifeless food hall. Consumption occurs, while being detached and alienated from process or presence. Seeking to reawaken that familiarity of aliveness, this project reconfigures a series of choreographed spaces taking place in the Grand Central Market.


A Series of Visceral Choreography

Today, contemporary dining has shifted from sustenance to spectacle. If we are no longer seeking for sustence, what desire are we actually looking to fulfill?
